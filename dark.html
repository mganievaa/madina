DARK MATTER

DARK MATTER is a speculative packaging project that confronts the hidden human cost behind everyday chocolate consumption. Commercial packaging often sells indulgence, heritage, and craft - yet rarely acknowledges the troubling realities of cocoa production, particularly the widespread use of child labor. Drawing on credible open-source data from the NORC 2020 Cocoa Report, the project reframes familiar chocolate branding into a platform for ethical confrontation. Across five product types each design exposes a different facet of exploitation in the cocoa supply chain. The packaging visualises real statistics, including: 1.56 million children involved in cocoa production in Côte d'Ivoire and Ghana 42% of child laborers using dangerous tools such as machetes 24% exposed to harmful agrochemicals A 62% increase in cocoa production from 2008-2019 paralleled by a rise in child labor despite global intervention efforts Rather than treating infographics as an add-on, each package integrates the data directly into its visual structure. Stylised cacao pods - sliced vertically or transformed into pie charts — carry the statistics within their form, making the information inseparable from the product. The design language adopts a muted brown-beige palette, minimalist typography, negative space and clean geometric illustrations - echoing the visual polish of high-end chocolate brands. This deliberate luxury aesthetic creates a striking dissonance: an exterior that feels elegant and desirable reveals deeply uncomfortable truths. This tension is heightened by chocolate's cultural association with childhood joy. The system subtly plays on this irony: one child enjoys a sweet, while another labors to produce it. At first glance, the data-integrated visuals may feel approachable, even playful, echoing packaging that appeals to families. Yet, instead of masking reality, the designs expose it - reframing the language of premium branding into a clear-eyed reflection on the human cost behind the treat.
